The Ganzi Promise

Ganzi was born from a simple belief: that the finest coffee in the world should reach your cup without barriers, without middlemen, and without compromise. Our journey begins in the misty highlands of Rwanda, where volcanic soil and equatorial sun create some of the most complex Arabica beans on Earth.

But coffee is more than agriculture — it is culture. In Poland, coffee has long been a ritual: slow-brewed, shared among friends, savoured without hurry. Ganzi bridges these two worlds, bringing Rwandan heritage into the warm embrace of Polish tradition.

Every bean we roast is a tribute to the farmers who nurture it and the community that brews it.

The Magic of Rwandan Coffee

Why coffee from the "Land of a Thousand Hills" captivates the world — and why Ganzi is its finest ambassador.

Volcanic Terroir

Rwanda's coffee grows at altitudes between 1,600 and 2,200 metres, on ancient volcanic soils rich in minerals. This unique combination of cool nights, ample rainfall, and porous earth creates dense, flavour‑packed beans. The slow maturation process allows complex sugars to develop, resulting in a cup that is bright, aromatic, and layered with nuance.

Heirloom Varietals

Most Rwandan coffee is Bourbon — an heirloom Arabica variety prized for its sweetness and complexity. Unlike mass‑produced hybrids, Bourbon offers delicate notes of jasmine, bergamot, red berries, and stone fruit. Ganzi works exclusively with cooperatives that preserve these traditional cultivars, ensuring every harvest is authentic and traceable.

Washed & Natural Processing

Rwandan producers excel at washed processing, where cherries are depulped, fermented in clean water, and dried on raised beds. This method highlights the bean's intrinsic brightness and floral character. Some lots are also natural or honey‑processed, adding layers of wild fruit and body. Ganzi selects each lot to match the perfect roast profile.

Flavour Profile

A typical Rwandan cup surprises with notes of orange blossom, red currant, black tea, and dark chocolate. Acidity is vibrant but rounded, body is creamy, and the finish is long and sweet. Whether you prefer a light roast for clarity or a medium roast for balance, Rwandan beans shine.

The Bridge: Poland & Rwanda

Poland has a deep coffee culture — from the traditional kawa po turecku (Turkish-style) to modern specialty cafés in Warsaw, Kraków, and Gdańsk. What do Poles love? A rich, full-bodied brew with stories behind it.

Rwanda, known as the "land of a thousand hills," produces some of Africa's most elegant washed Arabicas. By combining Rwandan raw materials with Polish roasting craftsmanship, we create a coffee that honours both worlds.

Our roasting philosophy respects the bean's origin: light to medium roasts for delicate florals, or darker profiles for those who love intense, chocolatey notes. There is a Ganzi coffee for every Polish coffee ritual.
