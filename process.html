From Rwanda to Your Cup

Every step of our journey is guided by respect for the bean, the farmer, and the craft. Discover how Ganzi brings you the finest specialty coffee.

01 / GROWING

Volcanic Highlands

Our coffee grows on the misty hills of Rwanda's Southern Province, at altitudes between 1,600 and 2,200 metres. The region's volcanic soil — rich in minerals and perfectly drained — combined with abundant rainfall and cool nights, creates ideal conditions for Bourbon Arabica trees.

Farmers in the Nyamasheke, Huye, and Rulindo cooperatives hand-pick only the ripest red cherries, ensuring exceptional sweetness and complexity. Shade trees protect the plants and promote biodiversity.

Varietals: Red Bourbon, Bourbon Mayaguez — known for their floral aroma and balanced acidity.
02 / HARVEST & PROCESSING

Meticulous Sorting

Each morning, cherries are delivered to the washing station, where they undergo rigorous hand-sorting to remove underripe or damaged fruit. We use fully washed processing — cherries are depulped, fermented in clean water for 12–24 hours, then washed and dried on raised African beds for 10–15 days.

For special lots, we also offer honey and natural processing, which enhance fruity notes and body. The result is a clean, vibrant cup that expresses Rwanda's unique terroir.

Processing methods: Washed (classic), Honey (sweet & complex), Natural (intense fruit).
03 / MILLING & EXPORT

From Cherry to Green Bean

After drying, the parchment coffee is rested for two months, then hulled, graded by size and density, and hand-sorted again for defects. The green beans are packed in vacuum-sealed GrainPro bags to preserve freshness during shipping.

From the capital Kigali, the beans travel by sea to Gdańsk, then by road to our roastery in Kraków. The entire journey is carbon‑offset, and we maintain direct relationships with every cooperative.

Traceability: Each bag has a QR code linking to the farm and processing date.
04 / ROASTING IN KRAKÓW

Small-Batch Craftsmanship

In our Kraków roastery, we roast every batch on a traditional drum roaster, with capacities of just 15–30 kg. We use a slow, controlled profile to highlight each bean's intrinsic character — never rushing, never hiding flaws.

Our roastmaster tastes every lot multiple times, adjusting temperature and airflow to achieve a perfect balance of sweetness, acidity, and body. We offer three profiles: Light (for floral clarity), Medium (balanced & versatile), and Dark (rich & chocolatey).

Freshness guarantee: All coffee is roasted within 48 hours of shipping.
05 / PACKAGING & BREWING

From Our Hands to Yours

After roasting, beans are rested for 12–24 hours, then packed in recyclable, resealable bags with one‑way valves. We include brewing tips and a roast date sticker. Whether you use a V60, French press, or espresso machine, Ganzi coffee reveals its full potential.

We also offer a subscription service that delivers freshly roasted coffee to your door every week or month — so you never run out of exceptional Rwandan coffee.

Brew like a pro: Every bag includes a recipe card tailored to the roast.

Quality & Sustainability

Every choice we make honours the farmer, the land, and your cup.

Direct Trade

We pay 30–50% above Fairtrade minimum prices, invest in community projects, and visit our partners twice a year.

Specialty Grade

All our lots score 84+ points on the SCA scale — only the top 5% of world coffee production.

Eco-Friendly

Compostable packaging, carbon-neutral shipping, and solar energy at our roastery.
